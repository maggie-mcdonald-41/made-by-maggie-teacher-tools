Transition Words & Phrases

Organized by purpose:

To Introduce a quotation:

  • According to [Author/Source], “...”
  • As [Author] states, “...”
  • In discussing [topic], [Author] observes, “...”
  • The passage reads, “...”

To Explain a quotation:

  • This means that...
  • Essentially,
  • This demonstrates...
  • This illustrates...
  • This shows that...
  • This supports the idea that...
  • This proves that...

To Add Information:

  • Additionally
  • Furthermore
  • In addition
  • Also
  • Not only that
  • Another key point is

To Show Cause & Effect:

  • Because
  • As a result
  • Therefore
  • Consequently
  • Thus
  • For this reason

To Compare/Contrast:

  • Similarly
  • In the same way
  • However
  • On the other hand
  • Although
  • Yet
  • While

To Sequence Ideas or Events:

  • First, second, third
  • Next
  • Then
  • Afterward
  • Eventually
  • Finally

To Emphasize:

  • Most importantly
  • In fact
  • Indeed
  • Without a doubt
  • Clearly
  • Above all

To Conclude:

  • In conclusion
  • To sum up
  • Overall
  • In summary
  • All in all
  • Ultimately
