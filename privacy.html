Privacy Policy

Last updated: December 01, 2025

MadeByMaggie.org provides digital teaching tools designed for educators and students. We value your privacy and collect only the minimum data required for our organizers, reading trainers, dashboards, and Google-integrated tools to function.

This policy applies to all Made by Maggie Teaching Tools, including but not limited to: the writing organizers (Argumentative, Opinion, Narrative, Conclusion Builder), the Reading Practice Trainer, the Teacher Dashboard, and all future tools under the Made by Maggie brand.

1. What Data We Collect (All Tools)

Email Address (Google Sign-In — Optional)

If you choose to sign in with Google, we request your email address. This is used only to identify your Google Drive files, support autosave, and allow you to resume or recover your work. It is never used for marketing or shared with others.

Saved Work

Depending on the tool, your work may be stored in:

  • Your browser’s local storage
  • Your own Google Drive (via Drive API autosave)
  • Your teacher’s dashboard session (Reading Trainer only)

We do not store your writing or organizer content on Made by Maggie servers.

Dashboard Usage Data

The Teacher Dashboard collects:

  • Session codes created by teachers
  • Student answer attempts tied to those session codes
  • Skill-category accuracy and timing
  • Optional class/section labels

No student names are collected unless a teacher manually enters them.

2. Tool-Specific Data Practices

2A. Writing Organizers (Argumentative, Opinion, Narrative, Conclusion Builder)

These tools may save:

  • Your essay text
  • Writing logs (timestamps, revision progress)
  • Autosaved versions of your work in Google Drive

Your files are stored only in your Google Drive or your browser — never on our servers.

2B. Reading Practice Trainer (Student View)

During a reading session, the tool collects only:

  • Your selected answers
  • Timing and skill-category performance
  • Any highlight actions (optional)

This information is visible only to the teacher who started the session. No student account is created and no personal identifiers are required.

2C. Teacher Dashboard

The dashboard stores:

  • Teacher-created session codes
  • Answer attempts submitted during that session
  • Skill breakdowns and analytics

This data is used only to help teachers review performance. It is never sold, shared externally, or used for advertising.

2D. Google Docs-Based Tools (e.g., Conclusion Builder Add-On)

These tools may request:

  • docs.currentonly — to edit the currently open Google Doc
  • drive.file — to save or update files created with the tool

These tools do not search, read, or modify other files in your Google Drive and do not access your email content.

3. How We Use Your Information

  • To support saving, editing, and restoring your work
  • To enable Google Drive autosave and version recovery
  • To provide teachers with reading-session performance data
  • To maintain proper functioning of the tools you use

We do not use your data for marketing or advertising.

4. Data Protection & Security

  • All communication uses encrypted HTTPS/TLS connections.
  • OAuth permissions are limited to the minimum required scope.
  • Your files are stored only in your Drive or your browser’s local storage.
  • No organizer data is stored on Made by Maggie servers.
  • Dashboard data is tied only to teacher-created session codes.
  • No data is ever shared or sold to third parties.

5. Student Privacy & Compliance

Made by Maggie tools follow COPPA- and FERPA-aligned practices:

  • No direct student accounts
  • No persistent identifiers stored across sessions
  • No student emails collected
  • No advertising or tracking
  • Teachers control how student data is labeled and saved

6. Your Choices

  • Use organizers without signing in
  • Remove files from Google Drive at any time
  • Clear browser storage to erase local organizer data
  • Delete dashboard sessions to remove student attempt history

7. Changes to This Policy

We may update this policy occasionally. The “Last Updated” date will always reflect the latest revision.
