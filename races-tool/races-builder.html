🏁RACES Constructed Response Builder 🏁

✨Supports student writing without generating answers for them. This scaffolded tool guides students step by step through the RACES strategy so they can practice turning reading into clear written responses. Teachers can use it with any text or passage, making it flexible for classroom instruction, test preparation, or independent practice. Student work automatically saves locally so they can continue writing across multiple days. Optional Google Drive saving and Google Docs export make it easy to store, review, and track student writing over time.

Used in sentence starters like: “In [Title], the author…”

👩‍🏫 Teacher Tips

Goal: Students use the question + text evidence to write a complete response using RACES.

Tip: Require evidence to be quoted or accurately paraphrased with a paragraph/line reference.

Example (Teacher model): In Fish Cheeks, the narrator feels embarrassed because she worries about how Robert will judge her family. For example, in paragraph 6 the text states, “I was stunned into silence for the rest of the night.” This shows that she feels deeply uncomfortable and ashamed during the dinner. Overall, this moment demonstrates how strongly she wants to fit in with American culture.

Important: This tool provides structure, starters, and feedback — it does not write answers for students.

RACES Strategy
Restate • Answer • Cite • Explain • Summarize
R — Restate

Turn the question into the first sentence of your response.

Teacher Tips (click to expand)

Example (Teacher model): If the question asks, “Why does the narrator feel embarrassed in Fish Cheeks?” you could start with: “In Fish Cheeks, the narrator feels embarrassed because she worries about how Robert will judge her family.”

A — Answer

Answer the question directly and completely. Write 1–2 clear sentences that respond to every part of the question.

Teacher Tips (click to expand)

Example (Teacher model): This is because the narrator feels embarrassed and she worries that Robert will judge her family's traditions and food. She wants to fit in with American culture and fears that Robert will think her family is strange.

C — Cite Evidence

Add a quote or accurate paraphrase from the text. Include paragraph/line if possible.

Teacher Tips (click to expand)

Example (Teacher model):

According to Fish Cheeks, “I was stunned into silence for the rest of the night.”

E — Explain

Explain how your evidence proves your answer. Connect it back to the question.

Example (Teacher model):

This evidence shows that the narrator feels deeply embarrassed because she becomes completely silent during the dinner. Her reaction reveals that she is worried about how Robert will judge her family and their traditions.

S — Summarize

Wrap up in one sentence. Do not add new evidence.

Teacher Tips (click to expand)

Overall, this demonstrates that the narrator feels embarrassed because she worries about how others will judge her family's traditions.
