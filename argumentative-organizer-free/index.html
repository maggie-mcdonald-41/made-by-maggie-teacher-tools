🎯 Claim 🎯

💡 Teacher Tip: This is your main opinion or stance — your guiding idea.

Start with your point of view based on the prompt. Think about rewording the prompt to show your stance. Later, you’ll choose evidence and reasons that support it.

Example:
Prompt: Should homework be required in schools?
Claim: Homework should not be required in schools

🎯 Claim

Make a clear statement that shows your position on the topic. This is not a full sentence.

🔍 Find Your Evidence 🔎

💡 Teacher Tip: This section is where you collect your best evidence — a direct quote that supports each of your reasons. Your reasons are what your body paragraphs will be about.

What kind of evidence?
Just the quote itself! Copy a sentence (or two) directly from the article that connects to your reason. No transition words yet — we’ll add those later.

Where does it come from?
If you’ve been given articles in class, use those. Choose a part of the text that proves your point or supports your thinking. Your teacher will tell you if you are finding your own resources.

Remember:
- Use quotation marks
- Keep it short and powerful
- Stick with your claim - You will create reasons from your quotes

Example:
Quote: “Students who get too much homework are more likely to lose sleep and feel overwhelmed.” Reason: the work is overwhelming

What’s a counterclaim?
A counterclaim is the other side of the argument — what someone who disagrees with you might say.

Why use it?
Addressing a counterclaim shows that you've thought carefully about both sides. It actually makes your argument stronger.

📌 Thesis 📌

💡 Teacher Tip: Your thesis is the most important sentence in your whole essay.

What is it?
It’s one clear sentence that includes your claim (your opinion or argument) based on the prompt and your reasons (the points you'll use to support your claim).

Why does it matter?
Everything else in your essay will come back to your thesis. It’s like your essay’s roadmap!

Example:
Thesis:Homework should not be required in schools because it increases student stress and reduces time for family and hobbies.
Claim: Homework should not be required in schools
Reason 1: it increases student stress
Reason 2: reduces time for family and hobbies.

    👋 Introduction 👋

    💡 Teacher Tip: Your introduction is your reader’s first impression — so you want to grab their attention and let them know what your paper is about.

    What goes in an intro?
    We use the **HIT strategy**: Hook, Issue, Thesis.
    • Hook: A creative or thoughtful opening to pull your reader in
    • Issue: A few background sentences about the topic
    • Thesis: Your claim and your reasons (already done!)


    Helpful Tip: Don’t worry about being perfect. You can always go back and revise your intro once you finish your body paragraphs.

    Example Hook:
    Imagine coming home from school, exhausted — and still having hours of homework to do.

    Your intro needs to H.I.T. 👊: Hook, Issue, Thesis

    Time to introduce your reader to the topic of your paper. Here you will give some background information on the subject you are writing.

    🏁 Conclusion 🏁

    💡 Teacher Tip: Your conclusion is your chance to remind the reader what your essay was all about — and leave them with a strong final impression.

    What to include:
    • ✅ A transition (like "In conclusion")
    • ✅ A restated version of your thesis (same idea, new words)
    • ✅ A call to action or final thought


    Pro Tip: Don’t introduce any new reasons or evidence. Just wrap it up clearly and confidently!

    Example:
    In conclusion, homework causes more stress than success. Students need time to rest, spend time with family, and enjoy their childhood.

    ✨ Closing Transition

    🔚 Start your conclusion with a transition and a clear sentence that relates to your position on the topic. Don’t copy your thesis exactly — and don’t introduce new ideas here.

    What is a call to action?
    A call to action tells the reader what they should think, feel, or do after reading your essay. It’s the “So what?” or “What now?” moment.

    Make it powerful:
    • ✅ Use strong, clear language
    • ✅ Connect it back to your thesis
